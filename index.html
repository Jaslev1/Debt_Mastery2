Common questions

Is my financial information safe? +
Yes. Nothing you enter is stored on our servers or shared with anyone. It's used only to generate your plan in this session. Close the browser and it's gone.
Is this legal advice? +
No. DebtSnap provides financial information and education — not legal advice. Our guidance is based on publicly available consumer protection laws. For complex situations, we'll tell you when a consumer attorney makes sense. Many work on contingency for FDCPA violations, meaning no upfront cost to you.
Will this hurt my credit score? +
No. DebtSnap doesn't pull your credit report and doesn't report anything to credit bureaus. Zero impact on your credit score.
What if I'm already being contacted by collectors? +
That's when DebtSnap is most useful. You have significant legal rights under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act that most people don't know about. Your plan will cover exactly what those rights are and how to use them.
What's the difference between the $49 report and the $69/month support? +
The $49 report is a one-time purchase: your complete 5-step plan, all dispute and negotiation letter templates, and a creditor-by-creditor guide. It's yours to keep permanently. The $69/month support is a separate, optional add-on that follows: ongoing coaching, call scripts, motivation check-ins, updated guidance as your situation evolves, and access to the full playbook library. You can get the report first and add support later at any time.
Is AnnualCreditReport.com safe to use? +
Yes — it's the official, federally-authorized source. The FTC and CFPB both endorse it as the only authorized site for your free annual credit reports. It's operated jointly by Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. You're entitled to one free report from each bureau per year, and since 2020 all three have offered free weekly reports as well.
