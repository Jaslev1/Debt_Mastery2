About DebtSnap

Debt relief shouldn't cost a fortune.
Or require a lawyer.

Why we built this

The traditional debt relief industry charges 15–25% of your total enrolled debt to do something you could largely do yourself — with the right information and support. A family with $40,000 in debt can pay up to $10,000 for that help.

We thought that was wrong. So we built DebtSnap.

We combined the latest consumer finance research — from the CFPB, academic behavioral economists, and consumer law practitioners — to create a genuinely personalized, step-by-step debt action plan that costs almost nothing.

"63% of student loan borrowers have difficulty making payments. 45% of lower-income borrowers qualify for $0 per month in repayments — and simply don't know it."
— CFPB Student Loan Borrower Survey, 2024

What makes us different

Most debt tools give you a budget calculator and a list of tips. DebtSnap does something different: it figures out who you are first — your financial situation, your behavior patterns, your goals — and builds a plan specifically for you.

We use a research-backed behavioral framework developed from analysis of nearly 400,000 real debt cases to understand how you relate to money. That's what drives every recommendation we make.

We also know — from the data — that pressure-based, threatening approaches make things worse for most people. Every part of DebtSnap is designed to feel calm, clear, and achievable.

Your privacy is protected

Answer 5 questions about yourself

Five short questions — not about your finances, but about how you relate to them. Do you know exactly what you owe? Have you been avoiding creditor calls? How do you feel when you think about your debt?

Why does this matter? Research from nearly 400,000 real debt cases shows that people respond very differently based on their behavior patterns. The same advice doesn't work for everyone. Your plan is calibrated to yours.

Common questions

Is my financial information safe? +
Yes. Nothing you enter is stored on our servers or shared with anyone. It's used only to generate your plan in this session. Close the browser and it's gone. We know trust is everything in this space.
Is this legal advice? +
No. DebtSnap provides financial information and education — not legal advice. Our guidance is based on publicly available consumer protection laws. For complex situations, we'll tell you when to consult a consumer attorney. Many work on contingency for FDCPA violations, meaning no upfront cost to you.
Will this hurt my credit score? +
No. DebtSnap doesn't pull your credit report and doesn't report anything to credit bureaus. Zero impact on your credit score.
What if I'm already being contacted by collectors? +
That's when DebtSnap is most useful. You have significant legal rights under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act that most people don't know about. Your plan will cover exactly what those rights are and how to use them.
What exactly do I get with the $49 report vs. the $39/month plan? +
The $49 report is a one-time purchase: your complete 5-step personalized plan, all dispute and negotiation letter templates, and a creditor-by-creditor guide. The $39/month subscription adds ongoing check-ins, updated guidance as your situation changes, and full access to the playbook library. You can start with the report and upgrade later at any time.
